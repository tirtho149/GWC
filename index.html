Best-In-Show Recognition

The 2025 George Washington Carver “I Can” Poster Challenge provided a platform for artists and scholars to engage with the enduring legacies of George Washington Carver and Frederick Douglass Patterson through visual storytelling. This competition sought to highlight their contributions to science, education, and social progress by encouraging innovative artistic interpretations.

We are honored to have our work recognized with the Best-In-Show award among a diverse array of submissions that demonstrated exceptional creativity, historical awareness, and artistic excellence.

This recognition underscores the importance of interdisciplinary approaches in preserving and interpreting historical narratives. By merging artistic expression with historical scholarship, the competition facilitated meaningful engagement with the contributions of Carver and Patterson, fostering a deeper appreciation of their impact on society.

Event Information

The official exhibition and award ceremony will take place as part of George Washington Carver Day, providing an opportunity for attendees to explore the winning submissions and engage in discussions on the intersection of history, art, and education.

  • Date: February 3, 2025
  • Time: 5:00 PM
  • Location: Durham Great Hall, Memorial Union,Iowa State University,Ames,Iowa,USA

This event serves as a valuable forum for reflecting on the enduring relevance of Carver and Patterson’s work while celebrating contemporary artistic contributions that bring new perspectives to their legacy.

For more information, please visit the George Washington Carver “I Can” Poster Challenge.
